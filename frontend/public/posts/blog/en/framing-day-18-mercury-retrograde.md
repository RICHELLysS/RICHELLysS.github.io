*October 13, 2020*

# Mercury Retrograde

*Published on October 13, 2020*

This piece is purely to vent, relieve stress, and console myself—recording the epic Mercury retrograde events that have recently destroyed my mentality, as a warning to my future self.

1. My computer broke. Took it for repair, but they said the motherboard is dead. All files and drawings lost. I know backups are important, but I didn't expect such a sudden failure. No time to back up. [First blood]

2. Bought a computer on Amazon, but after the transaction, my account was frozen because the official thought it was too large a one-time expenditure. Called customer service three times, sent three emails proving the credit card is mine—still not unfrozen. [Double kill]

3. Credit card was stolen. Had to freeze it. Was planning to go downtown to look for a computer, but now I can't buy anything. [Triple kill]

4. Temporarily using an old iPad as a computer. Very inefficient and uncomfortable. Didn't adjust my mindset in time, missed two assignment deadlines due to poor time planning. [Quadra kill]

5. Scheduled a BRP appointment but forgot to bring my passport. Next available appointment is two weeks later. [Penta kill]

6. Without BRP, can't book a police registration. [Hexa kill]

7. During quarantine, didn't go to the lab and got scolded by the professor. [Seven kill]

8. My writing gets heavily censored—either no descriptions below the head or no death descriptions. [Eight kill]

9. Rent is due again. [Nine kill]

10. A long-lost decadent depression. Journal writing stopped. [Ten kill]

Applause, congratulations on the godlike streak. I don't want to say "sleep it off, everything will pass" or "luck will bounce back." My mentality just crumbled in an instant, but I don't mean to spread negative energy. I'm just trying to adjust my mindset by writing these down. As a young adult who has just stepped onto an unknown stage, reverse-running the poison alone, carrying many expectations, always feeling confident that I can do everything well. Some things I did complete properly—visa, renting, calling customer service. But I can't do everything well. Whenever I encounter difficulties, I get depressed and want to escape. It's shameful. This may just be the beginning of all trials. When you start doing something, trouble comes knocking. Everything you do is bound in a chain of cause and effect. The only thing you can do is keep moving forward, learn to abandon sunk costs, think positively, grit your teeth through the psychological pressure. Whether you choose to sleep or study, the sun rises and sets as usual. Staying in your comfort zone and wasting time is one way to live; solving troubles one by one and waiting for new ones to arrive is another. I've read many articles about how to spend one's twenties. By twenty, a person's views have already formed. Unlike in your teens, when your mind is active and you absorb new things quickly, you lack drive and experience. So the best way forward is to keep trying, failing, and reviewing—fall down and get back up. I'm now flying toward that age. The first half of my life was uneventful and accomplished nothing. I was overly indecisive and hesitant, always wanting to predict the future. But then the butterfly effect and chaos theory told me the future is unpredictable—only a general direction, no precise route. The high-end game of 3D reality still won't let a salted fish player like me peek at the code. I'm too weak. When my mind sinks into the limbic system, my emotions start to crumble again. I'm too weak. But sighing about it every time doesn't change the fact that I'm weak. Too sensitive, too sensitive. Only forward, forward. Don't overthink, don't overanalyze. Don't use "recharging" as an excuse to spend lots of time on entertainment. Don't stay up late playing games to get a sense of achievement. Eventually, the current troubles will be solved—it's just a matter of time. Then a new batch of troubles will be waiting.

When I wrote the above, a lyric came to mind: "Reality has no reset button, no extra life, no paid boost, no delete-and-restart." That line sticks with me the most. It touches me deeply. Whenever I get emotional for no reason, I think of that line, and then I can continue doing things.
