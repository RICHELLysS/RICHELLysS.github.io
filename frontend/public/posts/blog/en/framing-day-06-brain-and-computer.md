# Brain and Computer

*Published on January 2, 2020*

The coordination of three brain regions allows humans to make different decisions in different environments, but computers cannot. The main reason is that computers lack a forgetting mechanism. The five senses are ultra-high-precision sensors, constantly reading information into the brain while awake. The brain analyzes this information and makes decisions, similar to a computer inputting into the CPU and outputting control. This process requires a large amount of memory. The human brain is an incredibly powerful processor. It compares information with corresponding memories, assigns weights, and draws conclusions. Computer algorithms also output results based on a series of instructions, but their weight algorithms are very rigid and cannot handle lightly weighted information. One minute of sensory information received by the human brain would crash the world's largest memory computer. The forgetting mechanism of the human brain pushes unimportant things into the subconscious during sleep. The metaphor of consciousness as the tip of an iceberg above water and the subconscious as the massive ice below is perfect. Scientists believe the subconscious records all information a person has received from birth to the present. How is such a vast amount of information stored in a small carbon-based container? This is an unanswered question. I personally lean toward a hypothetical concept from "The Ruins of Time": everyone's subconscious is connected and shared in another dimension, like countless fish in the sea. When a person sleeps, the subconscious takes over the brain, and dreams may be sharing others' memories. Another hypothesis is the brain in a vat: people's subconscious memories are stored in an unimaginable machine that also transmits sensory signals to humans. These derived hypotheses mostly don't affect daily life, but they can add readability to novels. Just like thinking about framing doesn't have much to do with improving grades—it's just fun to seek explanations and understand various phenomena in the world.
