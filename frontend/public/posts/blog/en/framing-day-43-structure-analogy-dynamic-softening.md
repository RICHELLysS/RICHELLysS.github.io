# Structure Analogy and Dynamic Softening

*Published on March 20, 2021*

While drawing birds, I realized two insights: first, understanding the structure of wings; second, abstracting the external shape for alienation (as shown in Figure 1). I try to apply these two insights to dynamic expression.

To illustrate structure analogy, let's invite special guest Yuzuru Hanyu as a demonstration (his skating dynamics are incredible—walking material). By understanding the structure of bird wings, analogize it to the human body, draw auxiliary direction arrows (Figure 2), then draw the general shape based on the direction (Figure 3), add feathers, open and close—achieving a stunning wing dynamic effect (Figures 4, 5). This is an example of using structure analogy for animal dynamics.

To illustrate structural softening, let's invite guest Ye Lingyun (his ultimate move "Ren Yue Jie" is truly unforgettable). This guest is a 3D animated character. If you extract the structure directly from the character, it's very stiff like a puppet (Figures 6, 7), lacking elasticity and explosive power, thus weak dynamics. But if you soften the structure—dimensionality reduction into a 2D character, freeing the softness of the lines—you can give "force" and add a sense of gathering momentum (Figures 8, 9). This is an example of using structural softening to express dynamics.
