# Unlicensed Chatter

*Published on January 28, 2020*

From a theory to a blueprint, to a model, to a project, to a product/building, to an inspiration, back to a theory—this is a classic industry cycle. This chain includes almost all industries, and the supporting qualities of all industries are roughly divided into four: creativity, analytical ability, negotiation ability, and action ability. Everyone's attributes can be represented by a four-pointed star, with heights determined by attribute points. People in the theoretical phase allocate the most points to analytical ability. In the modeling and construction phase, negotiation and action abilities take more points. Inspiration is determined by creativity points. Attribute points are extracted from a person's spiritual pillars, which vary by individual. Common spiritual pillars include family, games, anime characters, romantic interests, scientific interest, desire to make money, revenge, competitiveness, etc. These spirits transform into beliefs, providing attribute points. Spirit is a scalar, belief is a vector. Different spirits determine a person's industry inclination. I believe that most people construct their life's spirit and belief in their minds before age 18, which then convert into attribute points after 18 (like leaving the beginner's village). The remaining years are spent seeking, verifying, and realizing the beliefs formed before 18. Lucky ones find the meaning of life by firmly walking in their believed direction. Unlucky ones have their beliefs shattered—if they don't undergo spiritual reconstruction, they remain decadent forever. But those who successfully reconstruct their spirit become more resilient and anti-fragile than average. Based on this premise, let's discuss some phenomena. Under the exam-oriented education system, most people's belief before 18 is to get high scores, so they build a four-pointed star that maximizes analytical ability and minimizes creativity. After intense competition and entering university, they find that what they deeply believed before 18 is not entirely applicable to survival. Breaking a belief is painful, leading to a period of decadence—probably why many university students feel lost. During university, people reconstruct their spiritual belief system. Since the high-score model doesn't fully apply to the practice model, improving action ability becomes more important than analytical ability. But the advantage of this system is that it rewards those who successfully rebuild their spirit with anti-fragility, making them more composed when facing difficulties again. The attributes of the four-pointed star are related to the 16-personality test algorithm—another topic.
