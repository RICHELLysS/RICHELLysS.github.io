# Diamond World

*Published on May 15, 2020*

About my diamond grid space:

I remember a dream from childhood: walking on a bridge, looking at the scenery on both sides, when suddenly the sky twisted into a blue glass oil painting, as if it would shatter in the next second. A cacophony in my ears, people fleeing in panic, but I walked against the crowd toward the scenery. It was the most beautiful sky I had ever seen, and I still can't describe the shock.

Strangely, I remembered that feeling upon waking. This has happened multiple times: the warmth of reading a book in a hot air balloon above the clouds, the thrill of adventure in a crimson dead forest, etc. These dreams have existed since I was very young, and I still dream them, remembering the stories.

I used to be a very imaginative person, insensitive to external information, liking to project fantasies onto real scenes (probably because I didn't know how to communicate with others). At the bookstore, I'd spend all day in the manga section, reading everything, then constructing worlds and plots in my mind. I was cringey, but I liked adding a billion fantasy settings to real life: stepping on the right grid while walking to unlock a superpower, twirling my fingers to levitate objects, running fast enough to feel flight...

Back then, I learned through imagination. I could do well on exams without studying much, probably because I turned what I learned into weird skill points in my imaginary world.

I call the various scenes from my dreams "Diamond Grid Parallel Worlds."

As I grew up, my imagination faded, and those dream scenes appeared less often. Learning became boring. I thought I had become the boring adult I once despised. Time to face reality and stop daydreaming. Fantasy and reality must separate.

Recently, after spending a lot of time reading comics and catching up on anime (slacking off), familiar scenes reappeared in my dreams. Strangely, I remembered them and continued the plot from last time. After waking, I thought for a long time and reached a new conclusion: a person's growth is rooted in childhood experiences. Empty fantasies might be childish, but they are my only treasure. I used to try to get rid of fantasy and become more realistic, and for a while I succeeded, but my heart became empty and painful. No goals, no pursuits, just waiting to die. Every day was boring. Add the buffs of being accustomed to loneliness and not knowing how to socialize, I lost passion for everything, hope for everything, confidence in everything.

I realized that for me, spiritual needs are far more important than material needs. I could live all day on text, images, videos, and imagination, with little pursuit of other things. The most painful periods were spent relying on my imaginary world. My way of understanding the world is connected to my imaginary world. Even my learning methods are built on imagination. So I shouldn't think these worlds hinder my growth; rather, their existence proves my existence.

I call this inner world the Diamond Grid World. I've loved diamond shapes since childhood and have dreamed of them many times. Walking on the road, the bricks suddenly turned into diamond grids. If I step on them in a certain order, I fall into the corresponding world. These dreams made me obsessed with looking down and stepping on grids as a child (and got me labeled a weirdo). I always feel that the real world I exist in is also a grid world. If I die in this world, I'll wake up in a higher-dimensional world (no nesting dolls). That's why I've always thought that the so-called real world is just a game. The fictional world I set up in the second Framing Day incorporated this concept.

What practical use does this diamond world have? Recently, my imagination has returned after a long absence, helping with learning, drawing, and writing. Looking further, maybe I can use it as a backdrop for light games. But most importantly, it's a mapping of my understanding of this world and the source of all my spirit.

Here are some memorable scenes from my dreams:

1. The sky turned into a blue glass oil painting, raining blue paint, feeling like it would shatter in the next second.
2. A colorful hot air balloon above a sea of clouds. I don't know how long it will fly. Every time I open the book in my hand, it has different content.
3. A crimson dead forest with dark cyan crows on the branches. In the center, an abandoned laboratory wrapped in vines.
4. An endless corridor. The flowers on both sides keep changing, but the person waving ahead never changes.
5. Standing on the wreckage of a spaceship, seeing an orange planet without a name and a galaxy formed by countless satellites.
6. The city is still brightly lit late at night. My body is light as a cat, leaping from rooftop to rooftop, escaping.
7. A familiar subway station, completely submerged in water. Gills grow behind my ears. Everything is soft, like touching it would sink in.
8. A belt that can reverse gravity, adjusting gravity to take off vertically. After setting parameters, I fly to the atmosphere and find cloud dwellers.
9. Opening a door, the room is upside down. The walls are painted green. Through the window, I see walking birds and flying humans.
10. In a pure white space, a needle holds up a huge stone. The pure white space flips into a pitch-black space, with several black spikes hanging a doll (a nightmare world for me).
