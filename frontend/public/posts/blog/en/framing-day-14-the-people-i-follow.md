*May 26, 2020*

# The People I Follow

*Published on May 26, 2020*

"A rare emotional special edition. Since I am a CARAT, the 14th member of SEVENTEEN, I will write these words on the 14th Framing Day and the fifth anniversary of SEVENTEEN's debut, May 26."

Q1: Why do you like a Korean boy band? Just because they're handsome?

"There are so many handsome guys in this world. If it were only about looks, I couldn't like them for this long. I usually jump from fandom to fandom, getting bored and moving on. But SEVENTEEN is the group I'll stay at the bottom of the pit for my whole life."

Can you say why?

"The reasons are many and tangled. If I had to pick one characteristic of SEVENTEEN, it's 'teamwork.' In detail, as a former otaku, my standards for boy bands were rigidly: synchronized dancing, good singing, all handsome, harmonious within the group, fair stage distribution, good fan service, positive guidance to fans. The first boy band I stanned met all those requirements—SEVENTEEN. If such a boy band really exists, why aren't they more famous? Because they have no discussion value. They do everything perfectly, so the media can only report things like 'synchronized dancing,' 'explosive variety effects,' and 'visual collection.' Trolls can't troll, passersby aren't interested. Not everyone likes perfection. The more perfect a group seems, the more they'll be attacked if anything goes wrong. Back to the point: why is SEVENTEEN about teamwork? If you search 'unity' on Baidu Baike, the image of hands forming a circle is from SEVENTEEN's pre-concert cheer. Count them—13 hands, right? That's it."

Q2: Why is a 13-member group called SEVENTEEN?

"Because 13 members + 3 sub-units + 1 group = SEVENTEEN. The name doesn't sound cool compared to other groups, but it's exactly about how these 13 people work together in three sub-units to create an artistic work. If I had to describe my impression of this group: a boy band that sits around a campfire at night and slowly tells you the stories they've experienced. Every song is not for marketing but for conveying emotions. They write their own lyrics, compose, choreograph, even edit their own MVs and secure their own variety show appearances—truly all-rounded. Every song is written for fans, so the spiritual value far outweighs the material value."

Q3: Why do you stay at the bottom of the pit unwaveringly?

"Many say not to be too emotionally invested in a boy band. But I say, if you don't invest emotionally in SEVENTEEN, there's no point in following them. SEVENTEEN is meant to be followed with genuine emotion—whether you like them, dislike them, or rationally analyze them. Here comes group effect again. In SEVENTEEN, I see the ideal group interaction model: seeking common ground while preserving differences, helping each other. If you're lucky, you'll encounter this kind of group dynamic—maybe in a middle school sports meet, a high school pep rally, or just a small group with unparalleled cohesion. The source of this cohesion I call pure love—no other interests involved, just simple love. It can be said that each member of SEVENTEEN loves the others. It's not just 'good relationship.' They have feelings that others can't understand. Everyone's efforts are for the team and other members. Everyone loves the stage and their fans. This unique group dynamic forms a continuously positive feedback network, even influencing the way fans interact. That's why SEVENTEEN has so many group fans. When I'm full of negative energy, this network catches me. In just two years, I've received more love than in all my previous years combined. So the reason I stay in this fandom isn't just because they're handsome, dance in sync, have good songs, and are skilled. It's because I fell in love with their unique bond and their unreserved love for fans. It sounds mushy and not like something I'd say, but they've become my comfort zone. We say: 'Every SEVENTEEN fan wants to live as a member of SEVENTEEN.' They say: 'Every fan is the 14th member of SEVENTEEN.'"

Q4: SEVENTEEN's fifth debut anniversary—any thoughts?

"You said you've lived until now because you want to become our tomorrow. So I will meet you with my achievements. I will like you until you no longer love each other."

Today is May 26, 2020, the 14th Framing Day, SEVENTEEN's fifth anniversary.
