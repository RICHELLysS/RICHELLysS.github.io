# Playing Life as a Game

*Published on March 11, 2021*

Notion is a treasure of a note-taking software. Even without advertising fees, I'll promote it (not really). Recently I tried to create a game mechanism in Notion to increase the fun of life.

The game is divided into 5 modules: Game Start, Warehouse, Check-in, Small Projects, Big Projects.

In the "Game Start" module, there are four storylines: [Main] storyline, [Side] storyline, [Surprise] special storyline, [Void] fantasy storyline. And four map sections: [Flow] progress, [Thin] inspiration, [Think] records, [Direction] planning. These can be abstracted into 8 grids, each containing up to 4 columns. For example, in [Main], place Computer, Math, Programming, Writing, Learning, and each column can have its own tasks.

The game rules are simple: daily set 8 grids, push 4 columns. That means update the grid content every day, and select any grid to advance the tasks in its columns. [Flow] is a special grid containing tags representing other grids, recording progress. When a column's task progress reaches 100%, check it off in the "Check-in" module. [Main] holds learning tasks, [Side] holds interest-based learning tasks, [Surprise] holds special day arrangements, [Void] holds whimsical story and character settings, [Thin] holds creative inspiration, [Think] holds daily records, [Direction] holds future planning.

The "Check-in" module is only for checking in and claiming rewards. After 5 successful check-ins, you can pick a "small reward" from the [Rewards] module. You might have guessed: [Rewards] includes "small rewards" and "big rewards." "Big rewards" can only be claimed after completing tasks in the "Big Projects" module.

Where do rewards come from? The "Warehouse" module accumulates six items: wish list, book list, recipes, various questions, writing/drawing materials, and learning materials. Regularly abstract things from the wish list into [Rewards]. Human desires are endless, so there's never a shortage of wishes, hence no shortage of rewards.

Small projects are tasks with short time cycles, completable within a week, such as writing a small game or finishing a mind map for a subject. Big projects require months of effort, such as building a website from scratch or creating software.

The game mechanism is tentatively set. Recorded in the Framing Day, it needs to be polished through practice, training my own time management skills. To repeat, Notion is truly a versatile software. I plan to make it a family heirloom. If it could link to my computer and directly organize files for me, that would be even better (starting to go off-track).
