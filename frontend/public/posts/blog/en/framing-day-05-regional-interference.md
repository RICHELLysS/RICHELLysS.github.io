# Regional Interference

*Published on December 22, 2019*

I made a mistake last time. To be precise, human cognitive structure is located in the cerebral cortex area, called the cognitive region, which is interfered with by the limbic region, also known as the emotional region. Human decisions are not entirely based on the operational results of the cognitive structure because emotions interfere. Emotions are mainly affected by the environment, followed by induced emotions. Dopamine secretion is directly linked to emotional changes—let's assume they are proportional. When dopamine secretion increases, people tend toward positive emotions like excitement and happiness; conversely, anxiety and depression. Since human instinct tends to choose actions that increase dopamine, even if the cognitive system tells you that doing something can enrich your knowledge, if that thing lowers dopamine, the emotional system will propose the opposite decision. That's why you tell yourself to study but can't put down your phone. It seems my emotional system often beats up my cognitive system. The way to change this is to keep dopamine at a stable level so that the emotional system no longer interferes with the cognitive system's decisions. The quickest way to increase dopamine is to eat. So there is a basis for saying studying makes you fat (misleading). Thinking this way, if studying could increase dopamine, wouldn't it be easy to become a top student? There must be such people, but not me.
