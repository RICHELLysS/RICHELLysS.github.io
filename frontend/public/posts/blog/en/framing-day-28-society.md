# Society

*Published on January 25, 2021*

This entry represents my personal views, exploring Marxist ideas in a way that is correct and aligns with current realities.

Starting from 0D: A person's value and needs generally follow Maslow's pyramid, from material pleasure at the bottom to spiritual pleasure at the top.

Moving to 1D: The value of interaction between two people or groups is roughly summarized by game theory. Simply put, cooperation yields stable income, betrayal yields risky income. Assuming both fully understand this, ideally all competition becomes cooperation.

Rising to 2D: To protect each party's value, contract systems are established under decision-makers' trade-offs. Attributes beneficial to long-term development are defined as "good," harmful ones as "bad."

Rising to 3D: Based on value distribution among groups, communism and capitalism emerge. Communism defines spiritual value, capitalism defines material value. They are at opposite ends of a number line. Extreme communism becomes totalitarianism; extreme capitalism solidifies class. The relatively ideal state is a balance point on the number line, benefiting people in acquiring both spiritual and material nourishment.

At 4D, we apply this model to predict current trends. I believe Marxist thought is very worth learning because its predictions and critiques of capital are precise. The above groundwork is my attempt to describe the traps of capital in my own words (commonly known as the Feynman technique, or teaching to learn).

First, clearly recognize capital's ultimate goal: to extract profit from a commodity, accumulating money, regardless of other factors. "Reification" is very beneficial to capitalists. Why exclude other factors? Because when discussing purpose, the simplest method is to follow the 80/20 rule and chaos theory (less than 20% of factors determine an outcome; the rest don't affect the general direction). What is "reification"? Labeling everything with fixed tags to measure it with money. Why reify? To increase commodity production efficiency and use labels to generate profit. How do capitalists reify something? Similar to producing screws in a factory: specify conditions for employees in a given position; those who don't meet the standards are eliminated. In the face of reification, everything is assembly-lined, including humans. What's the harm? Human value is defined by capitalists. To generate more profit, you must move closer to the bottom—the more tool-ized people become, the more serious the Matthew effect. People indulge only in illusions created by capital; even their spirits are controlled by capital. They seem to have ample food and clothing, but actually they're just seeking brief dopamine stimulation within the reified group, their needs forged by capital, consuming forced material while claiming to enjoy it.

Extreme vicious cycle examples:

Signature sneakers → capital labeling → mass production → targeted ads → selling group cultural identity → quality decline. Luxury concept marketing → selling class identity → targeted ads → ordinary people overdrawing consumption. Unreasonable systems defining human value → selling anxiety over metrics → depriving individuality → exploiting then firing → jobs easily replaced by machines.

These examples aren't deep enough. The most terrifying thing about extreme capitalism is its control over people. With capital, technology can subtly interfere with people's choices for profit, making people increasingly value only money and short-term pleasure, losing willpower, losing creativity and actionability to look to the future, and losing the ability to think independently and empathize with others. Capitalists control spiritual and cultural direction through the internet, showing people only what they want them to see. The gaze effect means people addicted to the internet increasingly cannot see the wider world, their spirits becoming fragile, trapped in fantasy. This is also part of the reason why "fandom" thinking erodes the internet. Ultimately, it's falling into marketing images. Capital provides a platform for spiritual venting for poor tool-people who are exploited by consumerism and happy about it, thereby gaining traffic and wealth—no different from brainwashing.

After all this, how to change the current situation? The answer is: no solution. The macro environment is such; capital is indeed the lifeblood of current social development. However, thoughtful people realize that decisions are made by people. Human spirit cannot be defined. As long as people are not controlled by money, they can control money.

Proposed solutions based on the above: At 0D (individual spiritual level), emphasize the importance of universal education. At 1D (interaction relationships), enhance moral fairness awareness. At 2D (rules and regulations), regulate capital's monopolistic tendencies. At 3D (group interests), recite the socialist core values (suddenly red atmosphere). As for more 4D directions, I need to read more books (after all, as a single-celled organism that lives on spirit alone, I cannot deeply discuss capital without evidence).
