# Humans and Order

*Published on April 11, 2021*

Zero dimension:

Define an abstract concept: human essence and appearance can be represented by 0 and 1. Assume everyone's cognitive initialization is 0, and 1 is the external cognition a person presents. Numbers beyond 0 and 1 represent how many angles a person can see things from, also determining their cognitive dimension. Here, assume the initial number of angles is three (good development, bad development, current state). Based on the above definition, a circle with a polygon outside is a "human."

One dimension:

"Humans" can only act under "order." There is no true freedom; if there appears to be, it is forged. Low-dimensional "humans" can be recognized and shaped by high-dimensional "humans." Absorbing correct knowledge can raise dimensions.

Two dimensions:

Based on the above, discuss the eight decisive fields of "order":

[Engineering, Science, Education, Economics, Art, Literature, Law, Management]

Each field has a specific order model. Under this defined system, describe the eight fields:

Engineering: tools used by "humans"

Science: concepts proposed and defined by "humans"

Education: knowledge transmitted by "humans" to the next generation

Economics: transactions between "humans"

Art: "humans'" understanding of beauty

Literature: "humans'" expression of their thoughts

Law: "humans'" definition and handling of evil

Management: "humans'" management of "humans"

Three dimensions:

In this world, with limited resources, there are two kinds of "order": "public ownership" and "private ownership." "Public ownership" aims to reasonably allocate resources; "private ownership" aims to profit oneself. "Humans" who support a certain system form an "-ism." If the two systems contradict, opposition and conflict inevitably arise.

The cause of any conflict between "humans" is essentially cognitive difference—shortened to bias. Either different cognition of the shape of a thing causes bias, or high-dimensional "humans" attempt to shape low-dimensional "humans'" cognition, commonly called labeling.

Four dimensions:

I believe the above model can analyze many social problems. For example, selling anxiety: exploiting insufficient cognitive angles to sell products. IQ tax: using false cognitive angles to deceive those eager to raise dimensions quickly. Bias propagation: using media and single culture to obscure certain unseen angles. In fact, for all things, as long as "order" exists, low-dimensional "humans" create illusions due to constrained cognition. These have no substantial impact on high-dimensional "humans," because the advantage of high-dimensional "humans" is mastering a certain "order" to control a certain group. Here, the derogatory or commendatory nature of "control" depends on dimension. Low-dimensional "humans" wanting to escape control must hold onto the ability to think independently. Thinking is the most efficient way to determine future direction. Under the rules of this world, only thought is truly free—the "0" part. Beyond that, relative to "humans," the angles of seeing things are all determined by "order."
