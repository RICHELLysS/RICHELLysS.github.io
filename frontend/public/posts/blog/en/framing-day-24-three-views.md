# Three Views

*Published on January 16, 2021*

The three views are outlook on life, values, and worldview. Everyone's three views differ, so the paths they take differ, like each branch of a tree extending in different directions.

The purpose of inducing the three views is that when willpower is weak and there's no motivation for anything, willpower comes from faith. Here I define faith as the convergence point of all beliefs, and belief as the level of expectation for something to happen (similar to stocks, only in my personal understanding of stocks). With faith, one can see what is worth pursuing in life, beyond just money and power, thus forming one's own three views without blind conformity.

Q: What is faith?

Expressing my view within the above definition: practically, it's fuel for human action, obtainable internally or externally. There's no high or low, right or wrong—only belief or disbelief. Believing makes it meaningful. Blind group conformity to faith is undesirable; only referencing its purpose and sustainability is acceptable.

Q: What is my own faith? Broad faith category: design theory. Self-derived faith category: imaginative design theory. Internal cognitive faith: subjective theism.

Definition of outlook on life: how to live this life? My outlook: when young, question, strive, hit walls—ultimately find meaning in things. When older, teach. When even older, write books and draw comics.

Q: Definition of values and my values? Values definition: what is valuable?

My values: knowledge and problems are valuable; wealth created by my own mind is very valuable.

Q: Definition of worldview and my worldview?

Worldview definition: the significance of the individual to the group and the group to the individual. My worldview: patriotism first; realizing individual value enhances group value, and group value in turn motivates individual value enhancement.

Q: What is the purpose of writing Framing Days?

The purpose is to give myself a spiritual feedback and safe haven. Whenever I'm influenced by external information and start doubting and denying myself for no reason, I can review my own thoughts and walk my own path.

For me, writing down thoughts and sharing them publicly increases actionability. Although Mr. Wen Yiduo said "doing" always comes before "saying," sometimes "saying" helps me see myself more clearly, thus gaining motivation to "do." Determining and writing down the three views early reduces confusion and overthinking.

Recently, a quote inspired me:

God finds himself/herself by creating. — Stray Birds

Perhaps writing down these thoughts is a form of creation.
