# Imagination and Learning Perspective

*Published on December 22, 2020*

In the twelfth framing day, I mentioned my imagination, leading to a premise: my learning perspective is based on imagination. Since the word "imagination" is too abstract, I'll simplify it into the concept of association: an ability to connect things. It can be abstracted as a "line" used for learning. What does a "line" do? It connects any two things in the world. Combining previous cognitive thinking, learning something new is like a "line" connecting to a new "point." How to understand a "point"? Based on the Golden Circle, I propose my own concept: "what" is the basic concept of knowledge, "why" is determining the purpose of learning knowledge, and "how" is using knowledge for oneself. Now, this line has successfully connected to new knowledge (hooray!), achieving a process from nothing to something. Not long ago, I gained a deeper conceptual understanding of how computers work: computers can only read 0 and 1, controlled solely by different sequences of low and high potentials. Bringing this concept into my learning perspective for induction: everything in the world is composed of relative 0 and 1 (a bit abstract), meaning everything has an opposite. The definition of this opposite is the subjective difference in people's thinking frameworks: good and bad, good and evil, reason and emotion, programming and being programmed, object and object-oriented. Since people see things from different angles, the opposites they construct in their minds differ. For example, when you ask two people "What is the opposite color of red?" one says "green," the other says "blue," and they both have different explanations—one from a complementary color perspective, the other from a hot-cold sensory perspective. The opposite of "red" can be any color, only differing by subjective choice. This principle applies to learning perspectives. Returning to 0 and 1, these two numbers can abstractly explain everything in the world. In ancient Chinese saying: everything has yin and yang. "Yin" is 0, "Yang" is 1. Western and Eastern philosophies differ fundamentally because the West believes everything "exists," without "non-existence"; the East believes the opposite of "existence" is "non-existence," which arises relative to existence. Hence Chinese mathematics includes the "empty set." Thus "yin and yang generate each other" is a uniquely Chinese concept, and Chinese learning also emphasizes "Taiji," closely related to culture.

I seem to have digressed a bit. Returning to my learning perspective: it is constituted by imagination, meaning I easily go off track while learning and thinking, jumping from current content to unrelated content (commonly called inattention). So I added a new rule in my mind, using 0 and 1 to control thoughts. The following describes the scenes in my mind after psychological suggestion adjustment: 00: thoughts flow and jump between different things; 01: thoughts flow but stay only on the current thing; 10: thoughts are fixed but can jump between different things; 11: thoughts are fixed and stay only on the current thing. This constructs a rule for my imagination to better learn and continue framing.
