# Golden Circle

*Published on December 21, 2019*

I first learned about the "Golden Circle" concept in a TED Talk. Its function is to provide a sequence for building a stable thinking structure. The best order for a person to establish a new structural point is from the innermost circle to the outermost. Since new knowledge always needs to connect with existing knowledge, a new piece of knowledge requires a line to connect to the existing knowledge—that is, starting from the initial "why." Then connect the various contents learned about this thing, including formulas, applications, and examples, to the line—these represent "how." Put a ring around these things and label what they specifically are—that's "what." Finally, mark an arrow on the line pointing to other structural points—that's "where." Thus, a new structural point is created.

This establishment of cognitive structure allows people to learn new things quickly and even create new knowledge points. However, not all cognitive structures are connected; some consist of scattered knowledge points that do not form a diamond-like solid knowledge structure (aha, these structures can be analogized to chemical molecules). Thus, different people have different cognitive structures. This cognitive structure is also influenced by the structure of the human cerebral cortex, but that's a separate discussion.
