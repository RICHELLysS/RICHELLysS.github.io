# Designer

*Published on December 19, 2019*

The designer pushed open the studio door. The table was filled with well-known designers from all over, and the only empty seat was left for him. "You're late. These are the only materials you can choose from," said a person next to him, pointing to a basket floating a pitch-black spherical object.

"These materials are enough," the designer said with a confident smile.

"It seems the championship is no longer in suspense," some whispered.

The designer took the sphere back to the lab, meticulously carved it with unique tools, created a rough structure, and set the initial state.

"How should I design it this time? The opening must be stunning—let's just do a big bang," he muttered to himself.

"Those old fellows all like to inject supernatural powers into the universe, looking down on basic particles. How about..." The designer quickened his work.

On the presentation day, designers from all walks brought their unactivated universes to the venue.

"Hey, what new trick are you pulling this time?" a friend teased. The designer's universe looked unremarkable.

"All made of basic particles, nothing worth mentioning."

When the activated universe was placed in the venue, everyone present was shocked.

The designer once again surprised everyone. Not to mention the bold explosive initialization that refreshed the universe's construction speed and the rapid emergence of life signs, his cosmic life actually connected with the lives of other designers' universes.

"How is this possible? Basic particles with no supernatural properties!" stomped last year's runner-up in frustration.

"I gave my lifeforms a new attribute: imagination."
