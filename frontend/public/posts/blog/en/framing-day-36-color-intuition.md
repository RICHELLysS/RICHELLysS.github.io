# Color Intuition

*Published on March 5, 2021*

This survey aims to study people's subjective color perception of certain things. The "objects" range from macro to micro, from concrete to abstract. There are no correct answers. Respondents simply imagine the object when seeing the question and answer subjectively. If there's no problem with the above, please feed me data. Thank you.

As of today, 43 people have participated. I originally planned to collect 50, but having this many participants in the first survey is already gratifying. If you want to be friends, check out my WeChat moments. Enough nonsense, let's start analyzing randomly.

The approach of this survey:

I divide things into three categories: abstract cognitive things, personal cognitive things, and public cognitive things.

The survey includes:

Abstract cognitive things [broad concept, no entity]: mathematics (discipline), circle (shape), love (emotion). Personal cognitive things [narrow concept, has entity]: air and electrons (hard-to-perceive things), birds and fish (animals), buildings, floors, phones and laptops (visual sensory things), sound (auditory sensory thing). Public cognitive things [broad concept, has entity]: people, streets, sea, sky, universe (strongly present things). The relationship of these three categories is shown in Figure 1.

For the collected data, I only look at the proportions of the two most prominent options and extract them, as shown in Figures 1-3. It seems people favor black, white, gray, and blue (not really).

Based on image characteristics, people's color intuition of things can be divided into three types: unified, polarized, and diverse.

Define a concept: imagination deviation.

That is, people's deviation in color intuition for non-unified cognitive things. Preliminary hypothesis: the more non-unified the cognitive thing, the greater the deviation.

As for how to formulate and quantify this concept, I beseech the god of mathematics to descend upon me.

This analysis ends here for now. When the next phase of the survey begins depends on metaphysics and thinking about choosing the domain of definition and how to collect more participants.
