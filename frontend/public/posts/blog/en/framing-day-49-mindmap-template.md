# Mind Map Template

*Published on April 29, 2021*

Mind maps are the most convenient way to record divergent thinking (personal opinion). Extract useful information from the model manifested in the 48th framing day. One concept is one divides into two, two into four, four into eight. For my system, any object classification does not exceed eight. Subdivisions beyond eight are all in the realm of searchable materials and need not be specially defined. So when I build a model to solve a specific problem, the mind map I draw also follows this rule. Since I cannot understand overly complex things, when facing any situation, I try to generate a simplified mind map template close to this, as an initial analogizable point to determine direction (this seems to fulfill, in some way, the wish for rational planning from my zeroth framing day).

My approach: to build a model, two major modules are needed: theoretical practice and technology. So I align "0" with "theoretical practice" and "1" with "technology." Under this system, "theoretical practice" and "technology" complement each other and are also opposing. Subdivide "theoretical practice" into "modeling" and "experimental design." "Modeling" usually refers to mathematical modeling, but can be any conceptual modeling as long as the definition is clear and usable. "Experimental design" aims to test the rationality of the model; design ideas usually have references and are close to the problem itself. These two are also represented by "0" and "1." Further subdivision leads to objects with specific characteristics. The purpose of modeling is to describe and predict changes in objects. How to collect and analyze objects belongs to the experimental design part. The essence of "technology" in this system is to improve construction efficiency—to put it bluntly, to find ways to simplify problems, related to transformation. I've newly learned two terms: "safe transformation" and "heuristic transformation." "Safe transformation" means discovering existing "tools" and learning the rules to use them. "Heuristic transformation" means inventing new "tools" through hypotheses and continuous attempts. The difference is roughly: one finds a telescope, the other builds a new type of telescope (aha, the difference between engineers and scientists?). Both are still aligned with "0" and "1," respectively.

With the above analogizable structure, we can apply it to practical situations to quickly organize thoughts. Start diverging from "search engine." It can be divided into "search principle" and "data." "Search principle" can be further divided into "search algorithm" and "search implementation." "Data" can be divided into "collection" and "analysis/processing." "Search algorithm" typically includes nodes and layers. "Search implementation" typically includes depth-first and breadth-first search. "Collection" includes surveys and web crawlers. "Analysis/processing" needs to clearly express the characteristics of a certain group and the relationships between variables. After diverging once, you can tackle difficulties module by module. If you encounter a bottleneck, you can pinpoint which part lacks understanding.

Now, try diverging from the question "How to build a search engine?" to see if it matches the mind map template. To build a search engine, there are two paths: either reference existing frameworks, or innovate from the bottom up. Existing frameworks require studying professional articles. The most important thing is to understand the specific domain of the search and its analogizability to integrate into my own understanding system. Interface characteristics are naturally observable: what elements appear on a typical search engine page and which frontend parts they correspond to can be judged. The "innovation" part is harder to diverge. First, I need to define search: search is essentially using algorithm characteristics to induce and capture information from a bunch of strings. So a new search rule means creating a new linking rule. Based on my own definition and thinking about the "2048 rule," the corresponding programming modules "recursion" and "dynamic linked lists" seem to be involved more, at least providing some ideas. But innovation is still very impractical. So the "reference existing frameworks" approach is more feasible.

Through the above two examples of analogizable mind map templates, I find this really practical. The approach is gradually becoming clearer. From now on, I'll rely on it for divergent thinking.
